King Pelé

Brazilian Hero

Pelé, often regarded as the greatest soccer player of all time, a Brazilian legend

Here's a time line of Pelé's life:

  • 1940: Edson Arantes do Nascimento, known as Pelé, is born in Três Corações, Minas Gerais, Brazil, on October 23.
  • 1956: Begins his professional career with Santos Futebol Clube at the age of 15.
  • 1958: Wins his first World Cup title with the Brazilian national team, scoring 6 goals in the tournament.
  • 1960: Helps Santos win their first Copa Libertadores.
  • 1962: Wins his second World Cup with Brazil, though he played only part of the tournament due to an injury.
  • 1963: Santos wins their second Copa Libertadores with Pelé as a key player.
  • 1969: Scores his 1,000th career goal in a match for Santos.
  • 1970: Wins his third World Cup with Brazil, being one of the standout players of the tournament.
  • 1971: Announces his retirement from the Brazilian national team.
  • 1975: Starts playing for New York Cosmos in the NASL (North American Soccer League).
  • 1977: Retires from professional football after a farewell match between New York Cosmos and Santos.
  • 1981: Receives the title of "Athlete of the Century" by Sports Illustrated magazine.
  • 1995: Appointed as Brazil’s Minister of Sports.
  • 2000: Awarded the FIFA "Player of the Century" title, alongside Diego Maradona.
  • 2022: Passes away on December 29, at the age of 82, leaving an indelible legacy in world football.
